Case Study: The "Urgent Fee" UPI Scam

The Crime: UPI/Online Payment Fraud.

How it Happens: A college student, Rahul, received an urgent phone call from someone claiming to be from the university's accounts department. The caller stated that Rahul's recent semester fee payment had failed due to a server error and that he would be barred from the upcoming exams if a late fee of ₹500 wasn't paid immediately. Panic-stricken, Rahul agreed to pay. The scammer sent a "payment request" link via SMS. Instead of sending money to the university, the link was a collect request. In his rush, Rahul entered his UPI PIN, unknowingly authorizing a deduction of ₹50,000 from his linked bank account instead of the ₹500 fee. The scammer immediately disconnected the call and became unreachable.

Who is Targeted: College students are frequently targeted because they are highly active on digital payment platforms but can be easily pressured by threats related to academics, exams, or urgent administrative fees.

The Consequences: Rahul lost a significant portion of his family's savings meant for his education. Beyond the severe financial loss, the incident caused immense mental stress, anxiety, and a deep loss of trust in digital payment systems.
